01

A manifesto for the unremarkable.

We have made too many things. Too many shapes, too many edges, too many promises uttered into rooms that were already full. Nothing is the inverse practice. We sand it down. We sand the sander.

Each surface here is a refusal. Each line a long apology to your retinas. The product, should we ever ship it, will be the absence of itself — gift-wrapped, hand-numbered, sent nowhere.

Nothing is not a brand. Nothing is not a movement. Nothing is, at most, a Tuesday afternoon that forgot to introduce itself.

03

Process. Or the long way around.

i.

Removal

We begin with a thing and slowly, patiently, take from it. The thing forgives us.

ii.

Quietening

We turn the volume down on the remaining shapes until they hum at the frequency of forgotten dreams.

iii.

Calibration

An engineer in a grey shirt squints at a grey number for a grey afternoon. The number does not change. It is correct.

iv.

Disappearance

The piece is removed from itself. What remains is what was always there, only more so.

v.

Delivery

Nothing arrives in a box that did not exist. You open it. It opens you.

04

An essay on the absence of essay.

There is a particular kind of quiet that arrives only after every other sound has politely excused itself. We are interested in that quiet. We are, perhaps, only interested in that quiet.

Consider a room. Now consider the room without the room. Now consider what remains, which is, of course, you, and the long fluorescent hum of your own paying attention. That is the work. That is all the work.

“ To make Nothing is harder than to make Something. Something has demands. Nothing has only its terrible, quiet patience. ” — a person we did not interview

We could go on. In fact, we have been going on, in the next room, for several years now. You are welcome to listen. Bring nothing. We will match it.

Say nothing, back.

If you must, you may whisper it into the form below. We will not respond. This is the agreement.
